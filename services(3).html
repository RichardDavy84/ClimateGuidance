Expert Climate Services

High‑resolution data, intuitive tools, and training to help you understand and act on climate change.

Our mission is to bridge cutting‑edge climate science and real‑world decision making. We start with community‑standard CMIP6 global models and CORDEX regional models to ensure our projections are rooted in the best available science. To capture small‑scale variability and extremes we supplement these with convective‑permitting models and our own statistical downscaling software, KrigR1. With more than 15 years of research on Arctic climate change and extensive experience teaching and co‑developing tools, we help clients navigate data, understand its strengths and weaknesses, and turn insights into action. Below you will find an overview of our core services and how they create value.

High‑Resolution Climate Layers

  • Uses CMIP6 global and CORDEX regional models as the scientific foundation.
  • Supplements with convective‑permitting simulations for fine‑scale weather extremes.

We bring global climate science down to the scale that matters for you, removing bias and quantifying uncertainty so that plans are based on the best information available.

Frequently Asked Questions

What climate models and datasets do you use?

We base our projections on CMIP6 global models and CORDEX regional models – the community standards for climate research. To resolve small‑scale features and extremes, we incorporate convective‑permitting simulations and apply our own KrigR downscaling software to generate high‑resolution layers tailored to your region.

Why is high‑resolution climate data valuable?

High‑resolution data captures local variability, such as micro‑climates, extreme events and topographic effects, that coarse global models cannot resolve. This detail is essential for infrastructure planning, ecological management and adaptation strategies at regional to local scales.

What makes your downscaling approach unique?

Our statistical downscaling employs flexible kernels and optimisation strategies that are tuned to specific climate variables and available observations. The method is documented in peer‑reviewed articles (Davy & Kusch, 2021; Kusch & Davy, 2022) and continually refined with new data.

How do your workshops help clients?

We teach participants how to access, assess and interpret climate data. The emphasis is on understanding the strengths and limitations of each dataset and combining them effectively. Workshops build internal capacity so teams can continue exploring data independently.

How have your services created value for previous customers?

Our high‑resolution analyses helped park managers in Northern Norway adjust river management and plan for erosion risk. Our memos informed an environmental advocacy group’s policy campaign. Interactive tools we co‑developed with wind‑energy operators improved maintenance schedules and yield forecasting. These examples demonstrate how tailored data and tools translate into actionable insights.
